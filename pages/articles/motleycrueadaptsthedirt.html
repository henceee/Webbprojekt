Saturday 2 Nov. 2013

Mötley Crüe adapts The Dirt to film

Mötley Crew have announced that their best-seller autobiography The Dirt-Confessions of the World's Most Notorious Rock Band will be adapted for the silver screen.Mötley Crüe have hired TV and movie-director Jeff Tremaine, who previously has directed such shows as Jackass, Nitro Circus and Wildboyz as well as all of the Jackass movies for the adapation of The Dirt.

The book, which was released in 2001, was co-written by all four band-members and author Neil Strauss. It spent 4 weeks on the New York Bestselles list, and sold 13,000 copies in the UK. Neil Strauss has also co-authored Marilyn Mansons autobiography The Long Hard Road Out of Hell.

In an interview with Deadline.com, Tremaine says that his first attempt to score the gig, it was passed off as a joke.

"I’ve wanted to make this going back to 2001, when we were just planning the first Jackass movie and I found out that David Gale at MTV Films had just optioned the book. First of all, I had no idea how to make Jackass into a movie, but I said to him, let me direct that movie, too. He said, yeah, of course!

He was being sarcastic, because he had the same level of confidence in me as a director as I did at that time. Luckily for me, the movie never got made, and when this project became available, I put everything I had into chasing it and convincing everyone that I am the right guy for it. I really feel I am.”

Tremaine also pushes the importance of getting everyhing just right, but if you're hoping for a crazy and funny adaptation you might be out of luck. The director himself claims that the movie will be pretty dark, and will not be comedy.

“It’s important to get actors who play, or who understand how to deliver the charisma it takes to be onstage. Rock stars have a swagger. Some of what they went through is funny, but overall this movie is not going to be a comedy. It’s pretty dark.

I think fans of what I’ve done will like this movie, but it’s not going to make you fall out of your chair laughing.”

The script will also be polished by Californication-screenwriter and producer Tom Kapinos. The filming of the adaptation is planned for early next year.
